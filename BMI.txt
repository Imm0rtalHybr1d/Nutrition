Body Mass Index. 
It is a measure that uses a person's height and weight to estimate whether they have a healthy body weight for their height.
BMI is commonly used as a screening tool to categorize individuals into weight categories such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese.

Basal Metabolic Rate.
BMR is the minimum number of calories your body needs to function while you're resting. It's the energy required to maintain basic life functions like breathing, digestion, and blood flow. 
BMR accounts for 60 to 70% of the energy your body uses.Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to accomplish its most basic (basal) life-sustaining functions.
